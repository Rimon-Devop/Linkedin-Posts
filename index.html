After building 100+ WordPress websites (and earning $100k+ on Upwork), here's my plugin list that I use on 90% of projects. Most developers overcomplicate their stack. I keep it simple and focus on what actually works. MY MUST HAVE PLUGINS: 1. Elementor for visual building and design The most flexible page builder I've worked with. 2. WPS Hide Login for security First thing I do on every site. Hiding the login URL stops 90% of automated attacks before they start. 3. Rank Math SEO for search optimization Everything you need for technical SEO without the bloat. 4. WP Rocket for performance Best caching plugin, hands down. My sites consistently score 90+ on PageSpeed with this. PLUGINS I USE OFTEN: 5. Gravity Forms when clients need advanced forms More flexible than free alternatives and saves hours of custom coding. 6. WP Mail SMTP for reliable email delivery Not all hosts handle email well. This plugin fixes that in 5 minutes. 7. WP Activity Log when multiple admins exist Game changer for debugging. You can see exactly who changed what and when. 8. Custom Post Type UI + ACF for custom content Perfect combo for building custom solutions without writing a theme from scratch. This setup has helped me maintain a 100% job success score and Top Rated Plus badge. What plugins would you add to this list? Drop your recommendations below


For performance / caching: Instead of WP Rocket, I recommend using Perfmatters + Super Page Cache combo with Cloudflare. For SMTP, I would use Fluent SMTP works flawlessly and free version supports logging. For clients that don't have an easy-to-setup SMTP server (for example if they are using Office 365, then I recommend using SMTP2GO which has a dedicated WordPress plugin and very easy to setup, great for transactional emails, free up to 1000 emails per month) Instead of WPS Hide Login for security, I would use Wordfence and block / rate limit access to /wp-login.php and /xmlrpc.php using Cloudflare free plan's custom rules.


The WP Activity Log point is underrated. I've worked on sites where multiple people had admin access and tracking down who changed a setting or deleted content was impossible. Having a log of every action makes debugging faster and stops the "I didn't touch anything" conversations. It's also useful for catching plugin conflicts. When something breaks after an update, you can see exactly what changed and when.

How I found my first job on Upwork in just a few cover letters. But that was in 2018, and competition was different... I've earned over $100k on Upwork now, and today I want to share how it all started. Back in 2018, I was another freelancer trying to break through. Zero completed jobs, zero reviews, zero trust. My strategy was simple: aim for small projects, around $20-$100. I knew nobody would hire a freelancer with 0 jobs for big or important projects. After a few cover letters, I got my first project - converting a design from Adobe XD to HTML/CSS. I still remember this project clearly. I made extra features that the client didn't ask for, just to impress them. It worked well. But here's the thing nobody tells you: Getting the second project was HARDER than the first one. I thought after landing the first job, the second would be easy. I was wrong. It took me more than 2 weeks of daily monitoring, sending cover letters, and getting no responses. It was frustrating, but I didn't stop. My advice for new freelancers on Upwork: 1. Start with small projects, You need testimonials that prove you're reliable and won't disappear after 2 days. 2. Only apply for jobs you can do 80%+ confidently, Your first projects must be done 100%. You don't have the luxury to figure things out as you go. 3. Spend time on quality cover letters. Create hooks, demonstrate you understand the task, and show the client they can relax. Today I have 100% Job Success Score and Top Rated Plus badge (only 3% of Upwork freelancers have it).
